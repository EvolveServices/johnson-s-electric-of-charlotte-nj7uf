What We Offer

Residential Electrical Services

A complete range of home electrical services to keep your Charlotte home safe, efficient, and up to code.

Electrical Panel Upgrades

An outdated breaker panel is one of the leading causes of house fires. We upgrade older fuse boxes and undersized panels to modern, code-compliant electrical panels that meet your home's needs.

  • 100A, 200A & 400A upgrades
  • Fuse box to breaker panel replacement
  • Sub-panel additions
  • Full permit & inspection included

Outlets & Switches

Sparking outlets, dead switches, and overloaded circuits are warning signs that shouldn't be ignored. We install, repair, and upgrade outlets and switches throughout your home quickly and safely.

  • GFCI & AFCI outlet installation
  • USB & smart outlet upgrades
  • Outdoor & weatherproof outlets
  • Dead outlet diagnosis & repair

Lighting Installation

Transform your home's ambiance and energy efficiency with professional lighting installation. From recessed lighting to ceiling fans, chandeliers to under-cabinet lighting: we do it all.

  • Recessed & can lighting
  • Ceiling fan installation
  • Dimmer switch installation
  • Landscape & exterior lighting

Home Rewiring

Older homes with aluminum or knob-and-tube wiring present serious fire hazards. We safely rewire your home with modern copper wiring, bringing your electrical system up to current NEC standards.

  • Full & partial home rewiring
  • Aluminum wiring remediation
  • Knob-and-tube replacement
  • Addition & renovation wiring

Electrical Troubleshooting

Tripping breakers, flickering lights, burning smells: our experienced electricians diagnose and resolve electrical issues that other contractors can't figure out. We find the root cause, not just the symptom.

  • Tripping breaker diagnosis

Surge Protection

Protect your appliances, electronics, and home systems from power surges with whole-home surge protection. One properly installed surge protector at the panel safeguards everything in your home.

  • Generator transfer switch installation

Smart Home Wiring

Hardwired smoke and carbon monoxide detectors provide more reliable protection than battery-only units. We install interconnected detector systems that meet current North Carolina building codes.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions Charlotte homeowners have about our residential electrical services.

Do I need a permit for electrical work in Charlotte?

Most electrical work in Charlotte and Mecklenburg County requires a permit and inspection. We handle the entire permit process for you. This ensures the work is done to code and protects your home's value and insurance coverage.

How much does residential electrical work cost?

Costs vary by project. A simple outlet replacement may run $100-$200, while a full panel upgrade can cost $1,500-$4,000 or more. We always provide a detailed written estimate before any work begins, no surprises.

How long does a panel upgrade take?

Most panel upgrades are completed in a single day - typically 4 to 8 hours. We coordinate with your utility company for the brief service disconnect and schedule inspections to minimize disruption.

Can you work on older homes with knob-and-tube wiring?

Absolutely. We specialize in updating older electrical systems, including knob-and-tube and aluminum wiring. We assess the full scope of the work and provide a clear plan to bring your home up to current safety standards.

Do you offer same-day service?

Yes. We offer same-day and next-day appointments for most residential services, subject to availability. Emergency situations are prioritized and dispatched as fast as possible, any time of day or night.

What warranty do you offer on your work?

All residential electrical work performed by Johnson's Electric is covered by a 1-year written workmanship warranty. If any issue arises from our work within that period, we return to resolve it at no cost to you.
