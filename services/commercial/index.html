How Commercial Projects Work

Our Commercial Project Process

Larger projects need a clear process. Here's how we take a commercial electrical job from initial consultation to final inspection.

01

Site Assessment & Scope Review

A licensed commercial electrician visits your facility to assess the project scope, review plans, and identify any code considerations or utility requirements.

02

Detailed Written Proposal

We provide a comprehensive written proposal with itemized costs, project timeline, and scope of work. No surprises - everything is documented before a single wire is touched.

03

Permit Coordination

We pull all necessary commercial building permits and coordinate with the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) and utility company as needed.

04

Scheduled Installation

Work is scheduled around your business operations. We coordinate with your team and any other trades on the project to ensure a smooth installation.

05

Inspection & Sign-Off

All work is inspected and signed off by the appropriate authorities. You receive complete project documentation, including permits, inspection records, and warranty information.

Commercial FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from Charlotte business owners and property managers about our commercial electrical services.

Do you work after business hours to avoid disruption?

Yes. We offer after-hours, overnight, and weekend scheduling for commercial projects where business operations must continue. Additional fees may apply for after-hours work - we'll discuss this upfront in your proposal.

Can you handle electrical work during a tenant build-out?

Absolutely. Commercial build-outs are one of our specialties. We coordinate with general contractors, handle all electrical rough-in and finish work, pull permits, and ensure everything passes final inspection on schedule.

What size commercial projects do you take on?

We handle commercial projects ranging from small office suites to large retail and industrial facilities. Our team has experience with single-phase and three-phase systems, high-amperage service entrances, and complex multi-tenant buildings.

How do you handle commercial emergency calls?

We maintain 24/7 emergency dispatch for commercial clients. Electrical failures that shut down your business are treated as top priority, and we dispatch a licensed electrician as quickly as possible regardless of the hour.

Do you offer maintenance contracts for commercial facilities?

Yes. We offer preventive maintenance agreements for commercial facilities, including scheduled inspections, panel testing, and priority response for emergency calls. Contact us to discuss a plan tailored to your facility.

Are you insured for commercial work?

Yes. Johnson's Electric carries full general liability insurance and workers' compensation coverage appropriate for commercial projects. We can provide certificates of insurance to your general contractor or property manager upon request.
