Flooding, burst pipes, or roof leaks that expose electrical wiring create life-threatening shock hazards. We safely assess and restore electrical systems after water damage - do not attempt to restore power yourself.

Charlotte's severe thunderstorms, tornadoes, and ice storms can damage electrical service entrances, surge-damage appliances and electronics, or leave exposed wiring. We respond after storms to assess and repair electrical damage.

Emergency Service FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our 24/7 emergency electrical service in Charlotte.

What counts as a true electrical emergency?

True emergencies include: burning smells or smoke from outlets, panels, or wiring; visible sparks or arcing; complete power loss not caused by the utility; breakers that won't reset; electrical issues involving water; or exposed live wiring. When in doubt, call us - we'd rather help you determine it's not an emergency than have you wait on something that is.

How much does emergency electrical service cost in Charlotte?

Emergency service rates are higher than our standard daytime rates to reflect after-hours dispatch and rapid response. We are transparent about emergency pricing on the call - you'll know the rate before the electrician arrives. We never bill surprise fees.

Should I turn off my main breaker during an electrical emergency?

If you smell burning, see sparks, or suspect a fire hazard, turn off your main breaker and evacuate the area if needed. Call 911 if there is any sign of fire. Then call our emergency line. Our dispatcher can guide you through the safest steps for your specific situation.

Do you cover all of Charlotte and surrounding areas for emergencies?

We provide 24/7 emergency service throughout Charlotte and Mecklenburg County, including Uptown, SouthPark, Ballantyne, University City, Steele Creek, and NoDa. We also serve Huntersville, Matthews, Mint Hill, Pineville, and parts of Union and Cabarrus counties.

What if my power is out and I'm not sure if it's a utility issue?

Call Duke Energy first to check for outage reports in your area (1-800-777-9898). If they confirm there's no outage and power is out only at your property, it's likely an issue on your side of the meter - call our emergency line immediately.

Can you make a permanent repair during an emergency visit?

We carry a wide range of common parts and materials on our emergency trucks and can make permanent repairs on the spot in most situations. Complex repairs involving permits or special-order parts may require a follow-up, but we always leave your home in a safe condition.
