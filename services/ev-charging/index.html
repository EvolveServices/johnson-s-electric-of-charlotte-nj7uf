The Benefits of a Professional EV Charger Install

DIY EV charger installations and unpermitted work can void your vehicle's warranty and create fire hazards. Here's why professional installation matters.

01

Charge Overnight - Every Night

A Level 2 charger adds 25 to 30 miles of range per hour. Plug in at bedtime and wake up to a full charge - no more planning around charging stops or worrying about range.

02

Protect Your EV Warranty

Many EV manufacturers require professional installation for warranty compliance. An unpermitted or improperly wired installation can void your vehicle's battery or drivetrain warranty.

03

Safe, Code-Compliant Installation

EV chargers draw continuous high loads. A properly sized circuit with the right wire gauge, breaker rating, and GFCI protection is essential for safety. We get it right the first time.

04

Federal Tax Credit Eligible

The federal Alternative Fuel Infrastructure Tax Credit (30C) may cover 30% of your EV charger installation cost, up to $1,000 for homeowners. Ask your tax advisor about your eligibility.

05

Increase Your Home's Appeal

Home buyers with EVs increasingly look for homes with installed charging stations. A permitted Level 2 charger is a selling point in Charlotte's competitive real estate market.

06

Scalable for Multiple Vehicles

We design your EV charging installation with your future in mind - whether you plan to add a second EV, upgrade to a higher-amperage charger, or eventually add solar. Build it right from the start.

EV Charger FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about EV charger installation in Charlotte, NC.

How much does EV charger installation cost in Charlotte?

A typical Level 2 home EV charger installation (including the dedicated circuit and NEMA 14-50 outlet) runs $400 to $900. If your panel needs to be upgraded first, add $1,500 to $3,000. We provide a written estimate after assessing your specific situation.

Does my home's electrical panel need an upgrade first?

Not always. Many homes have sufficient panel capacity for an EV charger circuit. We assess your panel load during the site visit and let you know if an upgrade is needed. We can often add a 50A circuit to an existing 200A panel without any upgrades.

What's the difference between a NEMA 14-50 outlet and a hardwired charger?

A NEMA 14-50 outlet lets you plug in a portable EVSE, which is more flexible but slightly slower. A hardwired Level 2 charger (like a Tesla Wall Connector) is permanently wired for maximum performance and is the manufacturer's recommended option for most EVs.

Do I need a permit for an EV charger in Charlotte?

Yes. North Carolina requires an electrical permit for Level 2 EV charger installations. We handle the permit application, inspection scheduling, and final sign-off as part of our installation service.

Can you install a Tesla Wall Connector?

Yes. We install Tesla Wall Connectors (both Gen 2 and Gen 3) along with all other major EVSE brands including ChargePoint, Emporia, JuiceBox, Wallbox, and Enel X. We can also supply the hardware, just ask.

Is there a tax credit for EV charger installation?

The federal Alternative Fuel Infrastructure Tax Credit (30C) provides a credit of 30% of installation costs, up to $1,000 for homeowners. Commercial installations may qualify for a higher credit. Consult your tax advisor for details specific to your situation.
