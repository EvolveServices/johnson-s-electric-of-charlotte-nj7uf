Automatic Transfer Switch Installation

An automatic transfer switch (ATS) is the brain of your standby generator system. It detects the power outage, signals the generator to start, and safely transfers your home's electrical load from utility power to generator power.

  • Automatic Load Management Transfer Switches
  • 200A & 400A transfer switch installation

If you already own a portable generator, a properly installed manual transfer switch is the safe way to connect it to your home's wiring. It eliminates the dangerous practice of backfeeding power through extension cords.

How Installation Works

Your Generator Installation Step by Step

Generator installation involves electrical, gas, and mechanical work. Here's how Johnson's Electric coordinates all of it for a seamless experience.

01

Home Assessment & Load Calculation

We assess your home's electrical loads, your goals for backup power coverage, and available fuel sources (natural gas or propane) to recommend the right generator size.

02

Generator Selection & Proposal

We recommend the appropriate generator brand, size (kW), and configuration and provide a detailed written proposal covering all labor, materials, permits, and coordination.

03

Permits & Utility Coordination

We apply for the required electrical and gas permits. For natural gas generators, we coordinate with your gas utility for the service tap. Propane installations require tank placement coordination.

04

Installation Day

We pour or set the generator pad, install the transfer switch at your panel, run all conduit and wiring, and connect the generator to fuel, electrical, and controls. Gas connections are made by licensed gas contractors.

05

Testing, Inspection & Training

We test the complete system (manual start, automatic transfer, load testing) and walk you through the controls, maintenance schedule, and what to expect during an actual outage. Permits are finalized.

Most standby generator installations are completed in 1 to 2 days. Portable generator transfer switch hookups are typically done in 2 to 4 hours.

Generator FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about generator installation in Charlotte, NC.

How much does a whole-home generator cost in Charlotte?

A whole-home standby generator system (including hardware, installation, transfer switch, pad, and permits) typically costs $5,000 to $15,000+ depending on the generator size, fuel source, and complexity. Portable generator transfer switch hookups run $400 to $1,500.

What size generator do I need for my Charlotte home?

The right size depends on what you want to power. A 7-10kW generator handles essential loads (HVAC, refrigerator, lights, outlets). A 20-22kW generator handles most or all of a typical home. Larger homes with electric HVAC may need 26kW or more. We calculate your load during the assessment.

Can I run a generator on natural gas?

Yes. Most standby generators in the Charlotte area run on natural gas (if available at your address) or propane. Natural gas is preferred because you never run out of fuel during an extended outage, as it feeds directly from your gas line.

Do I need a permit for a generator in Charlotte?

Yes. Generator installations require electrical permits in Mecklenburg County and most surrounding jurisdictions. Gas line connections require additional permits. We handle all permit applications and inspections as part of our service.

Can you install a transfer switch for my existing portable generator?

Yes. If you already own a portable generator, we can install a manual transfer switch or generator inlet box that allows you to safely connect it to your home's wiring. This is much safer than using extension cords.

How do I maintain my generator?

Standby generators should be exercised weekly (most do this automatically) and professionally serviced annually. This includes oil changes, air filter replacement, spark plug inspection, battery testing, and a full system load test. We offer annual maintenance contracts.
