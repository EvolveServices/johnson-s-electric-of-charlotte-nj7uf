Electrical Safety Education

Electrical Safety Tips for Charlotte Homeowners

Electrical hazards are the third leading cause of home fires in the United States. As Charlotte's trusted licensed electricians, Johnson's Electric wants every homeowner to know the warning signs that demand professional attention.

01

Never Ignore Flickering Lights

Flickering or dimming lights — especially when appliances run — indicate loose wiring, an overloaded circuit, or a failing panel connection. These are fire hazards that require immediate inspection.

02

Tripping Breakers Are a Warning Sign

Occasional tripped breakers are normal. Frequent tripping signals an overloaded circuit, a short circuit, or a ground fault. Don't keep resetting — call a licensed electrician to assess the root cause.

03

Burning Smells Mean Stop and Call

A burning odor near outlets, panels, or switches is a code-red emergency. Turn off the breaker immediately and contact Johnson's Electric. Never wait on this one.

04

Two-Prong Outlets Are a Liability

Older Charlotte homes with ungrounded two-prong outlets lack modern shock and surge protection. Upgrading to GFCI-protected three-prong outlets is one of the best investments a homeowner can make.

05

Schedule an Electrical Safety Inspection

Homes 25+ years old should receive a professional electrical inspection every 5–10 years. We check panel condition, wiring integrity, grounding, AFCI/GFCI protection, and code compliance throughout.

Noticed Any Warning Signs?

Don't wait for a small issue to become a costly repair or a safety emergency. Our licensed electricians can diagnose and fix electrical problems fast.
